Dress Code

Semi-Formal / Santa Barbara Coastal Attire

We invite guests to wear shades of blue, neutrals, and soft coastal tones. To allow the bride and groom to stand out, we kindly ask guests to avoid wearing white, ivory, dark navy, or black attire. Portions of the courthouse grounds are grass, so comfortable footwear is highly recommended.

For Women

  • Tailored separates or pantsuits
  • Heels, elegant flats, or dressy wedges
  • Avoid overly casual sundresses or formal floor-length gowns
  • Cocktail dresses, midi dresses, elegant wrap dresses, or dressy jumpsuits

For Men

  • Collared dress shirts
  • No jeans or distressed denim
  • Tailored dress pants, slacks, or chinos
  • Dress shoes, loafers, or polished dress boots
  • Suits and jackets are welcome, but not required

Weather

Our Story

How we met

While out in San Francisco during a going-away gathering, William spotted a beautiful curly-brown haired girl across the bar. After talking for several minutes, Lissette realized there was something about William she could not quite ignore.

The rest is history…

Proposal

William likes to say he reversed engineered the Tokyo trip by planting seeds early, but Lissette already had a strange feeling they should look into visiting Japan during cherry blossom season. Little did she know, William had already been planning the proposal months in advance. Among the cherry blossoms on a quiet, sunny day at the base of Mt. Fuji, William started to recite a poem in Spanish. Afterwards, he asked Lissette to spend forever with him.

She immediately started crying and said yes.

Why Santa Barbara?

The ocean has always been significant to both of us, with Lissette having been born in the Dominican Republic and William in Southern California. Santa Barbara felt like the perfect place to celebrate this next chapter together: coastal, peaceful, and with the people they love most.
